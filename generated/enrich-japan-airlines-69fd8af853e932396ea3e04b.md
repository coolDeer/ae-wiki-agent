## Company Overview

[[companies/japan-airlines|Japan Airlines]] is a Japanese full-service airline group. This page was initialized from [[sources/meeting_minutes-69fd8af853e932396ea3e04b|SMBC Nikko's 2026-05-08 JAL meeting note]], which focuses on FY2026 guidance, fuel and FX pressure, capex, cargo, domestic aviation reform, ZIPAIR, and Mile Life strategy.

The current source suggests JAL's underlying demand is healthier than headline FY2026 passenger-plan declines imply. The major near-term issue is not demand collapse, but the timing and completeness of fuel-cost recovery through hedging, subsidy, and fuel surcharge mechanisms.

## Business Model

JAL earns primarily from domestic and international passenger flights, cargo, and adjacent mileage / loyalty businesses. The source highlights several profit engines: domestic business travel recovery, international fare increases outside China routes, strong cargo demand, and the Mile Life strategy that monetizes miles through financial-sector partners and premium redemption behavior.

ZIPAIR is the group's LCC growth asset at Narita. The source says [[companies/zipair|ZIPAIR]] had a difficult FY2025 but is recovering, with two additional aircraft planned in FY2026.

## Financial Summary

The source states that JAL's FY2026 profit target is ¥180 billion. Near-term earnings are exposed to fuel and FX volatility: at 160 JPY/USD and $200 oil, the monthly negative macro impact is estimated at ¥28 billion. After government subsidies, the monthly net negative fuel impact is ¥23 billion, while the July 2026 fuel surcharge offset is only ¥11 billion.

Capex remains heavy. FY2026 capex is planned at ¥350 billion, including ¥198.9 billion of aircraft investment. FY2027 capex is expected around ¥390 billion, while the latter half of the plan averages ¥450 billion annually. Aircraft investment peaks in FY2029 at ¥327 billion. Preferred dividends from subordinated convertible bonds are expected to be about ¥8 billion annually.

## Competitive Landscape

The main domestic comparison in the source is [[companies/ana-holdings|ANA]]. JAL's strategy differs from ANA in loyalty: JAL is targeting affluent customers through Mile Life rather than shrinking its high-status member base. In passenger aviation, the source suggests domestic and international demand are above plan, while China-route capacity reductions have improved supply-demand balance and supported fare increases.

In cargo, JAL benefits from strong long-haul demand and a new [[companies/cargolux|Cargolux]] Narita-Luxembourg codeshare that started in April 2026.

## Valuation

No explicit valuation multiple or target price was provided in the source. The investment lens should therefore focus on earnings bridge and cash-flow capacity: FY2026 profit resilience, subsidy decision, fuel surcharge recovery, capex burden, and timing of shareholder returns after debt redemption.

The key upside mechanism is H2 FY2026 recovery if fuel stabilizes or falls while surcharge recovery lags favorably. The key constraint is heavy aircraft capex plus subordinated debt redemption, including a ¥150 billion subordinated loan due in September 2026.

## Risk Factors

Key risks are fuel price volatility, Brent-Dubai basis mismatch, kerosene crack spread widening, incomplete fuel surcharge coverage on connecting itineraries, subsidy uncertainty, and high maintenance costs for older 767 and 737 fleets. The source says high maintenance costs may persist until those aircraft retire around 2028.

Demand risks include China-route volatility, regional-route price competition, and potential delay in domestic aviation reform. Capital-allocation risk is that heavy capex and debt redemption defer buybacks even if operating earnings recover.

## Catalysts

Near-term catalysts include the May 2026 government fuel-subsidy conclusion, the 2026-05-22 domestic aviation expert panel, Q1 FY2026 fuel-cost realization, July 2026 fuel surcharge implementation, cargo volume and unit-price persistence, and any restart of share buybacks after the September 2026 subordinated loan redemption.
