# Rationalising expectations

AMOLED DDI for Chinese smartphone makers is the key growth driver for Novatek in 2020. Therefore, the firm is not immune from the recent weakness in demand. On the flip side, the trend for upgrades in the specs for smartphone display continues, Novatek’s technology leadership remains solid, and its 5%+ cash yield is attractive. We maintain BUY for Novatek, keep the name in our yield enhancement portfolio, but lower our TP to TWD255 following a cut in our earnings estimates. Its 4Q19 results conference will be held on 11 February.

# Not immune from the demand weakness

We believe all major Chinese smartphone makers have recently revised down their 1Q20 shipment targets by 20% or more. For Novatek, Huawei’s downward revision from low-50m to low-40m (-30% QoQ/YoY), possibly because of the 2019nCoV outbreak and deteriorating overseas shipments as a result of a lack of GMS support, has the largest impact. Huawei not only accounts for \~80% of demand for Novatek’s AMOLED DDIs but is also Novatek’s biggest TDDI customer (\~40%). We therefore cut our assumption for AMOLED DDI units by 20-25% for 2020/21F. We also believe Novatek’s TDDI ASP is hard to sustain under this scenario, like what happened in 2Q19, though its GM should still improve given its 30%+ cost reduction. Our revised 2020/21F earnings are still 2-3% ahead of consensus.

# Still a stock with decent return potentials

Its 12-13x 2020/21F P/E and 5%+ cash dividend yield with 15% earnings CAGR in 2019- 21F is not demanding. Novatek’s technology gap to its rivals in AMOLED, TDDI etc is still significant. Most importantly, the option value for growth prospects such as optical fingerprint sensors, F-TDDI, 4k/8k TV conversion, etc also still exists. The nearterm hiccup is inevitable but Novatek remains a share to own after the adjustment. Our new TP of TW\$255 is still based on a 15x average 2020/21F P/E.

# Earnings estimates

# Cut 2020/21F earnings by \~6%

We cut our 2020/21F earnings by \~6% each to factor in the lower assumption for AMOLED DDI shipments and a more severe TDDI ASP decline amid soft demand. On the other hand, we have left our large DDI and SoC assumptions largely unchanged. Channel inventory for the former is low, and there should be still some 4k/8k TV conversion benefit this year, while the latter has been stable for 3 years.

One noteworthy thing is that we believe Novatek has leveraged lower-cost foundry partners for its legacy TDDI fabrication, and the firm benefits from a 30%+ cost reduction as a result. We believe therefore its TDDI GM will still recover to 30%+, close to the company average even with our more severe assumptions for ASP erosion now. This explains that our cut to earnings this round is mainly based on a cut to revenue rather than a downward revision of margins.

# Near-term: sales hiccup, but margins improvement continues

Novatek will provide its 4Q19 earnings results and 1Q20 guidance on 11 Feb after markets close. Sales of TWD16.5b (flat QoQ/+8% YoY) for 4Q19 already beat its guidance slightly, and we expect its GM/OPM to fall into the 30-32%/14-16% guidance range. Looking forward, we now expect 1Q20 sales to slide marginally (from a previous expectation of slightly QoQ growth) amid the weakening demand picture, but profitability will still improve thanks to a better product mix. Our revised 1Q20F and 2020/21F earnings are slightly ahead of the consensus estimates.

# Valuation and recommendation

# Maintain BUY, revise down TP to TWD255

We maintain BUY for Novatek but trim our TP to TWD255 (from TWD270), still based on 15x average 2020/21F P/E. We maintain our target P/E of 15x because our key investment thesis – upgrades to display for Chinese customers and Novatek’s technology leadership to capture business opportunities – remain valid. The shares are used to trading at this level (or exceeding 1x stdv above its average [14.4x]) when the firm has new growth driver(s), such as TDDI in 1H19. Despite the downward revision, AMOLED and TDDI will still act as the key drivers for 2020 and will both continue to be resilient into 2021, while new businesses (Apple, under-glass fingerprint, and surveillance camera processors, for example) will also kick-off in 2020 or 2021.

We understand it is hard to change investors’ perceptions about this stock. Nevertheless, we would also like to flag that the last time the share valuation exceeded 15x P/E was in 2Q19 when TDDI drove both Novatek’s sales and margins expansion. New products will drive both sales/earnings growth and valuation expansion, in our view. After a rationalisation of expectations, we believe the new product cycle (AMOLED in this year and multiple new products in 2021F) will still lead the shares trading at a higher multiple, with a 5%+ cash dividend yield as a downside protection.
