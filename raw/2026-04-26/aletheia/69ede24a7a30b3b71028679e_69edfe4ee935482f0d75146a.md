# Price Uptick

We raise TP for EZconn to NTD3,333 (from NTD3,000), maintaining a 26x FY27E PER multiple after reflecting upward earnings revisions. Our updated model incorporates an incremental uplift in switch-demand from robust general-server deployments, alongside a 10% optical-cable price increase in both 2026 and 2027. This adjustment reflects the \~35% rise in upstream optical-fiber material costs beginning in 1Q26, which is already permeating the supply chain and is expected to cascade into finished-cable pricing. EZconn remains one of our most compelling ideas, given its substantial exposure to the rapidly growing TPU supply chain and the strength of the ongoing switch-demand cycle. We now forecast EPS to expand fivefold between 2025 and 2027, positioning EZconn as the fastest-growing company within the global AI supply chain. Accordingly, we add EZconn to our Alpha Portfolio.

# Factoring in 10% price hike to reflect 35% rise in fiber price

Driven by an unusually strong surge in switch system demand—projected growth of 21% and 19% year-over-year in 2026 and 2027 compared to the conventional 5% to 7%—the optical cable market is experiencing meaningful expansion. This growth is further amplified by the migration from 400Gbps systems (12.8Tbps) to 800Gbps switch systems (51.2Tbps), which has increased optical fiber cable consumption by fourfold. These dynamics have created significant supply tightness for upstream optical fiber, leading to a 25%\~35% price increase in 1Q26 from key suppliers like Corning and Furukawa. We estimate this upstream pressure will result in a 10%\~15% price hike for finished optical cables (fiber accounts 50% of cost).

# Revising up 2026/27 EPS by 7%/11%

We revise up our forecasts for EPS in 2026/2027 by 7%/11% to NTD62 (+162% YoY) and NTD130 (+110% YoY) to incorporate the positive price change mentioned above and the potential positive impact of Top-of-Rack (ToR) switch demand, fuelled by robust general server deployment with +35%/ +21% in 2026/2027 by the top CSPs. In the near term, we expect 1Q26 EPS to exceed our prior estimate, supported by favorable currency trends and a likely better‑than‑expected operating margin. We raise our 1Q26 EPS forecast to NTD8.0 (- 19%QoQ/+90%YoY) from NTD6.50. In addition, we expect sales momentum to further accelerate in 2H26, following the ramp‑up of the company’s new capacity in the Philippines in 2Q26. We also see limited risk from optical‑fiber supply constraints, as the company’s major customer — one of the top CSPs — should help secure stable upstream fiber allocation.

# Upgrading TP to NTD3,333 but CPO is still too early

EZconn remains one of our top BUY ideas within both the TPU supply chain and the strong switch‑demand cycle. We raise our TP to NTD3,333 (from NTD3,000) still using 26× FY27E PER. However, our earnings do not include any contribution from CPO as demand visibility remains limited. Despite Nvidia’s aggressive capacity‑build plans for its optical‑engine supply chain in 2027–28, we believe CPO adoption still faces uncertainty given unresolved questions around reliability and the stability of data transmission.
