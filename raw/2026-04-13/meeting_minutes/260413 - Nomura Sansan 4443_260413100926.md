## AI Strategy and Product Development (AI Agent, MCP, AX)
- **Clarification on Sansan AI Agent vs. MCP Server**
  - Functional difference: The services are distinct and have opposite data flow directions.
    - MCP Server: An external-facing service that acts as a bridge (API) to take Sansan's data *out* for use with an external AI. It targets large enterprises already using their own AI agents.
    - Sansan AI Agent/AI Search: An internal-facing service that pulls external data (e.g., from Salesforce) *into* Sansan for analysis. It targets customers less experienced with AI agents.
  - Product positioning: The company intentionally separated the names to differentiate the services, as the service structures are fundamentally different.
- **Sansan AI Search and MCP Server Details**
  - Development status:
    - Sansan AI Search: Evolved from the Sansan AI Agent POC. It offers a standardized subset of the agent's functionality and is scheduled for implementation around summer 2026.
    - MCP Server: Currently in a trial phase with customers to refine features, which could take several months to a year.
  - Pricing model: Undecided for both services. The company is considering various models, such as including them in specific pricing tiers (e.g., Standard or Advanced plans) or offering them as paid add-ons.
- **Data Security and Database Enhancement**
  - Data ownership: Data pulled into Sansan via AI Search belongs to the customer; Sansan's own database is not enhanced by this, as it is combining two customer-owned databases within the customer's environment.
  - Security measures: The company implements extensive security measures for the critical data it holds, including employee certifications (e.g., Personal Information Protection Officer) and adopting industry-standard practices.
- **"AX" (AI Transformation) Initiative**
  - Definition: "AX" is a marketing term grouping various AI-powered functions and services across the company's products.
  - Examples:
    - For Sansan: MCP Server, AI Search.
    - For Bill One: Automated matching, automated approvals, automated data entry.

**Q: How does the company explain the significant volatility in Sansan's year-on-year order intake, which went from +20% in Q1 to -14% in Q2 and -30% in Q3?**
A: The company is not concerned about the negative growth in Q3 and attributes the volatility to several factors:
1.  **Price Optimization Effect:** The sharp decline in Q3 is primarily a rebound effect from a one-time price structure optimization that occurred in the previous fiscal year. The positive impact of that price change has now run its course, leading to a difficult year-on-year comparison.
2.  **Quarterly Fluctuations:** The business experiences considerable fluctuations from quarter to quarter, and the negative result in Q3 in isolation is not seen as an indicator of a negative underlying trend. The current level is not a cause for concern regarding the achievement of full-year targets.

**Q: What were the key business drivers and metric trends for the Sansan service in Q3?**
A: Key drivers and trends for Sansan in Q3 were:
1.  **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) per Contract:** Slipped slightly from 211,000 yen in Q2 to 210,000 yen in Q3. The primary reason is the successful acquisition of a large number of smaller-scale customers, which brings down the average.
2.  **Number of Contracts:**
    *   YoY growth was 13% in Q3, a slight acceleration from 12% in Q2 and 10% in Q1.
    *   The net increase has been steady at 300-400 contracts per quarter, and this pace is expected to continue into Q4. This is driven by strong acquisition of small and medium-sized customers.
3.  **Monthly Average Churn Rate:** The rate was 0.54% due to a one-time factor in March of the previous year. As the metric is a 12-month rolling average, this level is expected to persist for a while until that month is no longer in the calculation period.
4.  **Advertising Spend:** Advertising expenditure for Sansan decreased in Q3 compared to Q2. This was a strategic decision to reallocate budget and increase focus on Bill One. The amount decreased for Sansan was roughly equivalent to the amount increased for Bill One.

**Q: Can you clarify the distinction between the company's AI Agent, AI Search, and MCP Server products, including their purpose, status, and pricing?**
A: The products are distinct in their function and target audience:
1.  **Distinction and Purpose:**
    *   **MCP Server:** An external-facing service that acts as a bridge or API. It allows enterprise customers to take their data *out* of Sansan and use it with their own external AI tools. It is targeted at large enterprises already proficient with AI.
    *   **AI Agent / AI Search:** An internal-facing service that pulls data from external tools *into* Sansan for analysis. It is targeted at customers who are less experienced with AI. AI Search is a standardized version offering a subset of the functionality from the more complex, customizable AI Agent, which was tested on a Proof-of-Concept (POC) basis.
2.  **Data Security:** Pulling data into Sansan via AI Search/Agent does not enhance the company's own database; the data remains the customer's property within their environment. The company maintains robust security protocols for all customer data it handles.
3.  **Status and Pricing:**
    *   **Pricing:** The pricing structure for both MCP Server and AI Search is currently undecided. The company is considering various models, such as including them in higher-tier plans or offering them as paid add-ons.
    *   **Availability:** The MCP Server is currently in a trial phase with customers, which may last from several months to a year. AI Search is scheduled for implementation around the summer of 2026.

6.  **Unit Price:** The trend has been flat. An increase in the future is possible if there are new orders from large-scale customers or if new AI features are successfully priced and adopted.

    *   **Automated Matching:** Available since November, but the number of companies using it is still small.

    *   **Philosophy:** The company's core principle is to adopt the billing model that customers find most convincing and appropriate. The final pricing will be determined based on learnings from the trial and implementation phase.
