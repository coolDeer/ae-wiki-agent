**Q: What were the key points of concern from investors following the recent earnings announcement?**
A: The primary focus of investor questions was on the future, specifically the feasibility of the mid-term management plan. Key concerns included:
1.  **Achievability of Targets:** Investors questioned whether the company could realistically achieve its targets of 100 billion yen in profit and a 10% Return on Equity (ROE).

**Q: Why did domestic SG&A expenses increase by 1.8 billion yen year-over-year in Q4, even though the full-year figure showed a reduction?**
A: The Q4 increase is a result of a low comparison base from the previous fiscal year (fiscal 2024). During that period, sales promotion activities for the retail and e-commerce segments were intentionally restrained due to issues like fraudulent use. Consequently, the normalized spending in Q4 of the last fiscal year appears as a significant year-over-year increase.

**Q: What is the anticipated impact of the conflict in Iran on the international business, and what actions have been taken?**
A: The impact varies by country:
1.  **Thailand:** This is the country of most concern. Due to its high dependency on the Middle East, it faces the largest import burden and is highly susceptible to such shocks. If the situation deteriorates, the company may tighten credit screening and increase loan loss provisions.
2.  **Hong Kong & Malaysia:** As resource-rich countries, they are not expected to be significantly negatively affected.
3.  **Provisions:** The company already booked a provision in Q4 reflecting the situation with Iran.

**Q: What is the current business environment and outlook for the Greater China region?**
A:
1.  **Business Environment:** Domestic and inbound consumption are recovering, but the number of bankruptcies remains high. In response, the company shifted from a defensive risk-balancing stance to a more offensive one starting in Q4.
2.  **Performance:** The company has successfully grown its top line and is effectively controlling operating expenses. However, profit contribution showed no growth in Q4 compared to Q3 (+300 million yen).
3.  **Outlook:** While the top line is strong and the receivables balance is increasing, significant growth is challenging due to the market's relatively small size. The focus is on capturing inbound consumption needs and managing effectively within the difficult environment.

**Q: What is the current situation and outlook for the Mekong region?**
A:
1.  **Business Environment:** The situation remains challenging due to both the Middle East conflict and prolonged credit tightening measures. This has led to a decreasing receivables balance, making a quick business recovery difficult even if the Thai economy improves.
2.  **Performance:** Despite the difficult top-line environment, the company has improved its control over operating expenses (SG&A) and secured profit growth. The substantial profit growth in Q4 (from +3.1 billion yen at Q3 to +7.0 billion yen at year-end) was largely due to the positive impact of foreign exchange rates.
3.  **Outlook:** The market is viewed with caution, and a rapid rebound is not expected.

**Q: What is the current situation and outlook for Malaysia?**
A:

The significant positive impact on the provision for bad debts in Q4 (+1.2 billion yen) was due to a change in accounting standards related to the government's AKPK debt relief program, where certain receivables were reclassified from Stage 3 to Stage 2, resulting in a reversal of provisions.
2.  **Outlook:** Malaysia is expected to continue performing strongly and lead the company's overseas business.

**Q: What are the key drivers for the planned 13.5 billion yen decrease in domestic profit for the current fiscal year?**

1.  **Positive Factor:** An approximate **+10 billion yen** impact from the increase in the revolving credit interest rate.
2.  **Negative Factors:**
    *   A **-15 billion yen** impact from costs related to the next-generation system.
    *   An approximate **-3.5 billion yen** impact from a decrease in gains from receivables securitization (planned gain of ~10 billion yen this year vs. 13.5 billion yen last year).
    *   An approximate **-5 billion yen** net impact from other factors, including increased financial expenses (due to rising interest rates), a slight increase in provisions for bad debts, and higher sales promotion expenses.

**Q: What are the assumptions regarding interest rates and their impact on the current year's plan?**
A:
1.  **Rate Hike Assumption:** The plan incorporates the policy rate hike from December, which is expected to affect mortgage rates starting in May. It does **not** factor in any additional policy rate hikes by the Bank of Japan within this fiscal year.
2.  **P&L Impact:** Despite rising rates being fundamentally positive for the business long-term (due to variable rate loans), the net impact for this fiscal year is expected to be negative. This is because funding costs (e.g., higher deposit rates implemented in March) tend to rise first, creating a short-term lag before the benefits from higher lending rates are fully realized.

**Q: What is the future strategy for receivables securitization?**
A:
1.  **Scope:** The company will phase out the securitization of residential housing loans but will continue with the securitization of other assets, such as revolving receivables and investment-purpose condominium loans.
2.  **Financial Impact:** The company expects to generate around 10 billion yen in gains from securitization this fiscal year. This is not expected to be reduced to zero in the future.
3.  **Timing:** Since residential loan securitization (which was concentrated in Q4) is stopping, the gains will be more evenly distributed throughout the year going forward.

**Q: What is the breakdown and future impact of the 15 billion yen in system-related costs?**
A:

2.  **Next Year:** Depreciation expenses are expected to increase to about 10 billion yen as the system goes live in Q2. The one-time costs from the current year will roll off.

**Q: What are the main drivers for the planned acceleration in transaction volume and operating receivables growth this year?**
A:
1.  **Transaction Volume (planned +12%):** The primary driver is capturing a larger share of transaction volume within the AEON group ecosystem, which is still under-penetrated by the company's own payment methods. The newly released "AEON Pay" app will be heavily promoted at AEON Malls, drugstores, and My Basket stores to convert customers from using third-party payment methods (like PayPay) to AEON Pay.
2.  **Operating Receivables (planned +7%):**
    *   Growth will be driven by the increased transaction volume from the AEON Pay strategy.
    *   Installment credit, which has been growing significantly, will be a main driver.
    *   The bank will increase its focus on unsecured personal loans, such as card loans, home renovation loans, and education loans, an area that has not been a major focus recently.

**Q: What are the reasons for the projected 1.7 billion yen decrease in overseas operating profit for the current fiscal year?**
A: The decrease from 41.7 billion yen to 40.0 billion yen is entirely due to conservative foreign exchange rate assumptions made when the budget was prepared last autumn. At that time, the yen was expected to strengthen significantly. For example, the assumed rates were HKD at ~18 (currently ~20.4) and THB at under 4 (currently ~4.96), creating a large discrepancy with current rates and a potential for upside of over 3 billion yen if current rates hold.

**Q: How will the company achieve its mid-term plan of 100 billion yen in profit by FY2031?**

    *   **~18 billion yen** from non-organic factors: ~7 billion yen from the roll-off of one-time system costs and 11 billion yen from cost structure reforms (e.g., ATM network optimization).
    *   **~17 billion yen** from organic growth, primarily driven by the expansion of the domestic operating receivables balance.
3.  **Overseas Growth (from 40 billion to 60 billion yen):** The 20 billion yen increase will be driven by organic growth from the expansion of the receivables balance in cash cow markets (Malaysia, Hong Kong, Thailand) and growth in focus countries (Vietnam, Cambodia).

**Q: What is the breakdown and purpose of the 250 billion yen mid-term investment plan?**
A:
1.  **Overseas (110 billion yen):** Primarily for Malaysia, including investments in the AEON 360 joint venture (loyalty programs, data business) and the digital bank to drive business expansion and receivables growth.
2.  **Domestic (80 billion yen):**
    *   **AEON Pay:** To drive top-line and receivables growth.
    *   **AI:** To improve productivity and reduce costs in areas like call centers, collections, and credit screening accuracy. The efficiency gains are factored into the 11 billion yen cost reduction target.
3.  **Security and Safety (60 billion yen):** This is a necessary investment for which a direct financial return is not expected.

**Q: What is the shareholder return policy, particularly the dividend payout ratio, during the mid-term plan?**
A:
1.  **Target Payout Ratio:** The company maintains a target payout ratio of 30% to 40%.
2.  **Payout Trend:** The ratio is expected to be higher, near 40%, in the early years of the plan (this year and next) due to a lower profit base. As profits grow in the latter half of the plan, the ratio is expected to trend down towards 30% to maintain a stable dividend increase. The final year's payout ratio has not been determined.

**Q: How does the company plan to fund its significant working capital needs from investments and receivables growth?**
A: The primary funding source will be the expansion of the bank's deposit balance. The company is intensifying its focus on growing deposits to shift towards more stable funding and reduce its reliance on securitization. Key to this strategy is making the AEON Bank account more attractive through measures like revamping the "My Stage" loyalty program to encourage customers to use it as their main payment-linked account.
