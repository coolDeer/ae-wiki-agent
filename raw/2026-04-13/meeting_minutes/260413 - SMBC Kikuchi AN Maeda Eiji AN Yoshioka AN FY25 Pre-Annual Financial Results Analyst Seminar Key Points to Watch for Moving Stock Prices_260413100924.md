## AI Disruption: Impact and Market Sentiment
- **Divergent Views on AI Disruption between the US and Japan/Europe**
  - **US Debate:** A dominant view is that AI will make human roles redundant, rendering many SaaS products obsolete. This debate is considered somewhat unique to the US corporate and industrial structure.
  - **Japan & Europe Perspective:** There is a lack of a tangible sense that this is happening. Many Japanese executives are not fully engaged with these global trends as their operations are primarily domestic.
- **Market Sentiment and Overreaction to AI News**
  - The US-centric AI disruption narrative heavily influences global markets, causing stock declines even in seemingly unaffected companies (e.g., M3 in Japan falling after an Anthropic announcement).
  - This is perceived as an overreaction, likely driven by AI-driven trading algorithms programmed to sell software stocks on any AI-related news.
  - The expectation is that this excessive impact will fade, and a more rational discussion will emerge, as few investors truly believe software or SaaS is finished.
  - Current stock valuations are at or near historical lows, similar to the post-Lehman Shock period, suggesting a potential market bottom.
- **Two Primary Ways AI will Impact the Software Industry**
  - **1. Software Replacement by AI Agents:**
    - Unlikely to have a major impact on Japanese software, especially transaction-processing systems where errors are unacceptable (e.g., brokerage order systems, e-commerce fulfillment).
