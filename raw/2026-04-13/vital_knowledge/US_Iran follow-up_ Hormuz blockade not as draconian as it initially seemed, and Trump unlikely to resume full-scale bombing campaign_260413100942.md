US/Iran follow-up: Hormuz blockade not as draconian as it initially seemed, and Trump unlikely to resume full-scale bombing campaign

- The Pentagon issued a statement clarifying Trump’s blockade proclamation, and the language seemed to soften what the president posted earlier Sun morning – the US will block any ships “entering or departing Iranian ports and coastal areas” but will permit other boats to transit the waterway . What initially looked like a complete halt to all traffic now looks like it is focused only on Iranian vessels .
- A WSJ article posted at ~6pmET had an ominous title (“ Trump weighs limited strikes against Iran after peace talks collapse ”), but the real takeaway is that the president seems to be pivoting away aggressively from a resumption of the full-scale bombing campaign conducted over the last several weeks, which means the odds of a ground troop deployment are even lower than before . Some might look at this and fear that Trump is stuck – he can’t escalate, and he can’t exit , and there’s certainly some truth to that. But the fact that kinetic escalation looks to be off the table (for now) should help prevent the conflict from spiraling (although the situation is still extremely fluid, and should Iran fire on an American Navy ship, the whole war could easily reignite).
- A Washington Post report at ~4:30pmET said the Pakistan meeting might not have yielded a deal , but it did help foster an environment of goodwill, which has led the White House to feel that Iran “may still come to accept their terms” .
- None of this is to say that the Pakistan meeting was good – ideally a rough consensus would have been reache d on the major areas of disagreement, with ongoing talks explicitly scheduled. But it’s not accurate to say the negotiation process “collapsed”, and Trump seems to be pivoting aggressively away from kinetic escalation (even the Hormuz blockage isn’t as draconian as it initially seemed just a few hours ago ). Our view is still that the ceasefire will hold .
