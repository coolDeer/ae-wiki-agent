**Q: Regarding the 60 billion yen guidance, when was it finalized and why was it conservative compared to market expectations of 70 billion, especially given strong orders?**
A: The guidance was finalized for several reasons:
1.  **Timing**: The disclosure plan was finalized about 4-5 days before the earnings announcement, with internal discussions continuing until the last minute.
2.  **Conservative Stance**: The company prioritized certainty by factoring in the maximum possible risks. While recent order intake is strong, the guidance does not include this potential upside.
3.  **Factored-in Risks**:
    *   **Top Line**: Uncertainty about how geopolitical situations, like in the Middle East, might affect demand from the second quarter onwards. This was incorporated as a broad-stroke risk buffer.
    *   **Profit Side**: The possibility of rising costs for petroleum-derived components and logistics has been included. Quantitatively, if these risks were not present, there would be a potential upside of just under one percentage point in profit margin, equivalent to about 5-6 billion yen. This buffer is a general provision for potential risks rather than being based on a specific breakdown of cost increases.

**Q: What is the current order situation, particularly the trend in the fourth quarter and March?**
A: The order situation is as follows:
1.  **Fourth Quarter (Month-over-Month)**:
    *   **Company-wide**: Orders saw a single-digit percentage increase in December and January, and were flat in February.
    *   **AC Servos**: Showed double-digit percentage growth in December and January, and were nearly flat in February.
2.  **March (Start of New Fiscal Year)**:
    *   **Company-wide**: Order growth accelerated to double digits.
    *   **AC Servos**: The growth rate was higher than the company-wide average.

**Q: Has the recovery in orders become more widespread across regions and applications?**
A: Yes, the recovery has broadened significantly:
1.  **Regional Spread**: In the fourth quarter, AC servo orders grew in all regions, with all but one region showing double-digit growth.
2.  **Application Spread**: The recovery is no longer limited to specific areas like semiconductor-related demand for inverters; it feels more widespread.
3.  **China Exception**: China was the only region with single-digit quarter-on-quarter growth. The strength there is concentrated in specific applications like machine tools (driven by AI-related investments such as data center server racks) and semiconductors, while other areas remain weak.

**Q: Why is the capital expenditure (CAPEX) forecast for this fiscal year surprisingly high at 58 billion yen?**
A: The CAPEX remains high due to several large, ongoing projects:
1.  **Yamato Plant**: A portion of the project's investment carries over into this fiscal year.
2.  **DX Investments**: Includes YDX-related investments and the costs associated with an ERP system replacement. The investment amount for the ERP system in fiscal 2025 was about 6 billion yen.
3.  **US Investments**: Investments in the United States are set to begin in earnest.
4.  **Minami-Yahata Headquarters**: A portion of the investment for the systems engineering headquarters will fall into fiscal 2026.

3.  **Oil & Gas**: This market is more volatile but currently strong, driven by geopolitical factors. The company holds a #1 market share with its MV1000 Matrix Converter in this segment.
