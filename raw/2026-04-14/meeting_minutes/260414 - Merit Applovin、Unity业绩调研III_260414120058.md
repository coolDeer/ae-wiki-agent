# QA总结

**Q: What is the advertising budget share for the specified platform (AppLovin), and why is it so low?**
A: The budget share is very small, accounting for less than 0.5% of the total advertising budget. The primary reason is that the main overseas advertising spending is concentrated on major platforms like Google and Meta.

**Q: For your gaming clients, how do they typically work with platforms like AppLovin and Unity?**
A: Many gaming clients work directly with the platforms' own direct sales teams. They typically only use our agency to advertise on these platforms when they aim to create a broader media mix as part of their overall strategy. This is especially true for Unity, which has deep connections with game studios through its game engine, giving them an advantage that agencies cannot compete with.

**Q: AppLovin's quarter-over-quarter (QoQ) growth was about 6% in Q4 and accelerated in Q1. What are the main reasons for this acceleration?**
A: The acceleration in Q1 is attributed to at least three factors:
1.  **System Optimization:** The platform's own recommendation system is continuously being optimized, leading to improved data optimization capabilities and greater precision.
2.  **Ad Tool Evolution:** Its advertising tools, which were previously very DSP-like, are gradually becoming more brand-oriented.
3.  **Client Diversification:** The platform has expanded its client base beyond its core gaming clients. It is now attracting advertisers from categories such as web fiction, short dramas, tool-based apps, and e-commerce, leading to a more diverse client portfolio.

**Q: You mentioned e-commerce clients have not had a smooth experience on AppLovin. What are the specific reasons and shortcomings the platform faces in this sector?**
A: The challenges in the e-commerce space are due to the following reasons:
1.  **Sub-par Performance:** When e-commerce accounts were first enabled around Q1 of last year, the actual performance was generally below the industry average. While categories like 3C electronics were acceptable, traditional sectors such as home goods performed significantly worse.
2.  **User-Audience Mismatch:** The platform's core user base is heavily skewed towards ACG (anime, comics, games) and niche vertical categories. While this audience is high-quality for the gaming market, it does not align well with the needs of broad-based e-commerce, resulting in a lack of audience breadth compared to platforms like Google.
3.  **Perception of Traffic Quality:** The traffic, primarily from rewarded videos and gaming content, is sometimes perceived by e-commerce clients as "tail-end" inventory. Consequently, many advertisers prefer to either work directly with the platform or find higher-quality traffic on Google to drive their conversions.

**Q: In Q1, AppLovin launched an AI-powered ad creative feature called Spark. Have you used it, and what is the market's reception?**
A: Yes, we have used Spark. However, its reception has been mixed for a few key reasons:
1.  **Redundancy for Large Clients:** Many large advertisers already have sophisticated in-house or external processes for creative development, especially for platforms like Google. For them, Spark is often seen as a redundant tool that adds complexity rather than value.
2.  **Limited Cross-Platform Utility:** While the feature's data-driven feedback and optimization loops are meaningful, their utility is severely limited because they are confined to the AppLovin ecosystem. It cannot be used across different advertising channels.
3.  **Low Adoption Rate:** Due to its limited utility and perceived redundancy, the adoption rate for Spark is likely not very high at this time.

**Q: What is your outlook on AppLovin's ability to succeed in e-commerce advertising, and what are the main challenges it faces?**
A: From a technical standpoint, AppLovin has the potential to succeed. It is actively learning from platforms like Amazon by building similar API-driven interfaces that allow for easy ad launching with linked product catalogs. However, it faces significant challenges:
1.  **Intense Competition:** The e-commerce advertising space is dominated by deeply entrenched players like Google and Meta (with its Advantage+ series), which have long-standing, solid relationships with major e-commerce platforms like Amazon.
2.  **High Barrier to Entry:** For AppLovin to gain market share, it must provide a clear and significant value-add that is compelling enough for advertisers to shift their budgets from established platforms.
3.  **Short-Term Outlook:** While there is an opportunity given the rising cost and fraud issues on other platforms, it is unlikely that AppLovin can catch up or make a major impact in the e-commerce sector in the short term.

**Q: How is Unity's overall performance for advertising, and how does it compare to AppLovin?**
A: Our advertising volume on Unity is far smaller than on AppLovin. Its performance and characteristics are as follows:
1.  **Growth:** While there is QoQ growth, the absolute volume is so small that the growth is considered more of a volatile fluctuation rather than sustained, positive long-term growth.
2.  **Cost and Client Base:** The platform is perceived as expensive. This deters clients with smaller budgets or those who are highly cost-sensitive. As a result, only a few core, key clients actively advertise on it.
3.  **Competitive Position:** Compared to AppLovin, Unity's story and applications are considered weaker. While its game engine gives it a strong advantage in securing ad inventory directly from developers, its operational capabilities are seen as less savvy or slower than AppLovin's.

**Q: There appears to be a significant jump in Meta's year-over-year (YoY) growth from ~8% in Q4 to a reported 20% in Q1. Can you confirm this figure and explain the rapid growth?**
A: It is correct that Meta experienced strong growth in Q1. The growth was driven by two main factors:
1.  **Business-Side Shifts:** We shifted some advertising spend away from Google to Meta due to certain corresponding factors.
2.  **Platform Improvements:** Meta itself is implementing many changes on the content side that have contributed to growth.
Regarding the specific 20% YoY growth figure for Q1, I need to double-check my data to confirm its accuracy. I will provide a written confirmation to the coordinator.

**Q: What was TikTok's growth rate in Q1, and what is its projected share of your budget?**
A: TikTok's performance and budget share are as follows:
1.  **Q1 Growth:** It achieved double-digit growth in Q1. However, this is largely because it was starting from a very low base due to poor prior performance. The growth is not considered high-quality due to the small absolute numbers and ongoing advertising restrictions.
2.  **Budget Share:** The projected budget share for 2025 is between 8% and 10%. We do not anticipate the share will increase excessively beyond this range in the second half of the year, as expanding on the platform has proven challenging.

**Q: How effective is TikTok's AI-powered automated ad delivery feature compared to competitors like Meta and Google?**
A: TikTok's AI features have some unique strengths but are otherwise on par with competitors:
1.  **Smart Delivery System:** It offers a smart delivery system where advertisers can set a cost cap and total budget for automated ad delivery, similar in function to Google's Performance Max.
2.  **Unique Creative Advantage:** Its most distinctive feature is in short video creation and smart editing. By leveraging its massive library of creative assets and templates, it provides a unique advantage over other platforms.
3.  **Standard Features:** Other AI-driven features, such as its live-streaming pilot program and in-campaign intelligent account analysis, are now considered standard in the industry. They are comparable to what global platforms like Google have offered for a long time and are not considered a key differentiator.
