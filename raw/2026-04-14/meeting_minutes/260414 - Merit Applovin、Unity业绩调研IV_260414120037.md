## Key Performance Metrics for Gaming Campaigns on AppLovin
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**
  - Trend: CTR has shown a significant and continuous increase, rising from over 70% in October 2023 to around 90% by January 2024, and has remained stable at that level.
  - Reason: The platform is heavily utilized for running playable ads (PAs), which naturally leads to higher user interaction and clicks.
- **Conversion Rate (CVR)**
  - Trend: CVR has been consistently decreasing, creating an inverse relationship with CTR. It dropped from approximately 1.5% in October 2023 to 1% or lower in Q1 2024.
- **Cost Per Mille (CPM)**
  - Fluctuation: CPM is generally high, ranging from 300 to over 400. It experiences seasonal spikes, particularly during major e-commerce events like Black Friday, which creates intense competition for ad inventory and drives up costs for the gaming sector.
- **Cost Per Install (CPI) & Return on Investment (ROI)**
  - CPI Stability: CPI has been relatively stable, fluctuating in a narrow range (e.g., from 45 in Oct 2023 to around 40 in Q1 2024 for a specific product), as it is influenced by the entire user acquisition funnel (CTR, CVR, creatives).
  - ROI Performance: ROI has remained largely stable quarter-over-quarter, with a slight decrease of about 10% for one product, which was attributed to the product's performance rather than the channel itself.

## Unity Performance and Strategic Developments
- **Q1 2024 Performance Review**
  - YoY Growth: The actual YoY growth for Q1 was likely below the forecasted 15%.
  - Share of Ad Spend: Unity's share remained close to the previously stated 6-7% of the total ad budget, similar to its previous standing. Its share did not see a significant increase.
- **Strategic Corporate Changes**
  - Service Consolidation: Unity is reportedly shutting down its IronSource mediation service to focus on its core engine business.
  - Business Divestment: There are industry rumors that Unity plans to spin off its game publishing division, Supersonic, a move similar to AppLovin's strategy of shedding non-core ad/engine businesses.
- **Advertising Product Enhancements**
  - D28 ROAS Campaigns: Unity has launched D28 (Day 28) ROAS campaigns in open beta, an improvement over the previous D7 focus. This is crucial for SRG games with long payback cycles (12-15 months).
  - Improved Targeting: The new campaigns aim to capture high-LTV users who start spending significantly after D7, leveraging the improved Vector algorithm.
  - Automated Bidding: The platform now allows setting D28 optimization goals, with AI automatically adjusting bids based on a user's predicted 28-day value.
  - Faster Campaign Starts: Advertisers can now launch ROAS campaigns directly, bypassing the initial CPI campaign phase and shortening the "learning phase."
- **Performance Comparison and User Quality**
  - ROI vs. AppLovin: Unity's overall ROI and other metrics are generally 15-20% lower than AppLovin's.
  - User Quality Tiers:
    - Tier 1 (Broad): Google and Facebook attract general entertainment users.
    - Tier 2 (Game-Focused): Unity targets users already within a gaming context (casual to hardcore).
    - Tier 3 (Core Gameplay-Focused): AppLovin attracts the most relevant players by matching them to specific core gameplay mechanics via playable ads.

## Macro Environment and Industry Trends
- **Impact of Geopolitical and Economic Factors**
  - Russia: The absence of Google has increased reliance on third-party payment systems (e.g., Yandex) and ad channels like AppLovin. Ad review processes are longer. The Russia-Ukraine war led to a sharp drop in Russian user spending power.
  - Middle East: The conflict involving Iran has had a manageable impact on revenue, as Iran is not a Tier-1 market with high spending power compared to other regional countries.
- **Platform Service Fee Reductions**
  - Context: Google and Apple are reducing their service fees (e.g., from 30% to 25% or less) partly in response to economic pressure and competition from third-party payment providers.
  - Impact on Ad Spend: The 5% savings from fee reductions do not directly translate to increased ad spending. Ad budgets are strictly ROI-driven and based on payback models. The savings might shorten the payback period, but budget increases only occur if the overall model shows strong returns.
- **Meta's Audience Network**
  - Perception: The re-enabling of the Audience Network is seen as a defensive move by Meta to keep traffic and attribution within its ecosystem, rather than a significant new opportunity.
  - Impact on Spending: It has not led to an increase in spending on Meta. The focus remains on core campaign types like MAIA, AEO, and VO, which target paying users on Meta's primary properties.
- **Rise of TikTok Mini-Programs**
  - Opportunity: TikTok is seen as the next major growth area, comparable to the early days of mini-games on Douyin and WeChat in China.
  - Key Advantages: TikTok is currently not taking a commission and offers T+1 payouts, which is highly attractive for developers' cash flow. It also simplifies user acquisition by removing the need for a separate app download.
  - Impact on Budget: Ad spend will definitely increase on TikTok. This budget will primarily be reallocated from domestic Chinese mini-game platforms (Douyin, Kuaishou, WeChat).

**Q: What is the forecast for AppLovin's YoY growth in the second quarter and for the full year?**

1.  **Strategy**: AppLovin's strategy is focused on its core business, having absorbed significant volume after shutting down its own gaming operations.
2.  **Strengths**: They are particularly strong in playable ads. Advertisers prefer using AppLovin for playables, while using Google and Facebook more for AEO (App Event Optimization) and VO (Value Optimization) campaigns.
3.  **Performance**: Current performance is considered stable.
4.  **Growth Dependency**: Future growth potential is highly dependent on their ability to acquire a sufficient supply of external ad inventory, as they operate as an ad network.
5.  **Outlook**: It is difficult to predict a specific growth number, but performance is not expected to be worse than it is currently.

**Q: You mentioned "WS" is reducing its volume. Could you explain what this means?**
A: "WS" is a three-year-old game that has entered a mature or post-peak phase, leading to a reduction in its advertising budget. The reasons are:
1.  **Diminishing Returns**: The quality of newly acquired users is steadily declining, and the game is facing diminishing returns on its download volume.
2.  **Shift to Harvesting**: The game is now in a "harvesting period."
3.  **Ad Spend Reduction**: The ad spend ratio has been cut back significantly. For example, last year the ratio was 20% to 30% of monthly revenue, whereas this year it has dropped to 10% to 15% due to the increased difficulty of user acquisition.

**Q: Why was AppLovin's actual Q1 budget share (10%) lower than the initial expectation of 12%?**

3.  **Shift in Creative Strategy**: There is an ongoing shift towards using more AI-generated creatives and running more AEO/VO campaigns. This reduces the budget for creative production (from ~25% down to <10% of total ad budget) and lessens the reliance on playable ads, which are AppLovin's key strength. As a result, AppLovin's share has stabilized around 10%-12%, adjusted based on the product's life cycle.
