**Q: Could you clarify the competitive dynamics and your involvement in CoWoS versus CoPoS technologies?**
A: The situations for CoWoS and CoPoS are distinct:
1.  **CoWoS (Chip on Wafer on Substrate):**
    *   **Technology:** It is a wafer-based solution. Technologically, it is similar to normal wafer handling.
    *   **Competition:** When CoWoS started, TSMC utilized its existing automation suppliers, Rorze and Hirata, who remain the two primary players.
    *   **Our Business:** Almost 100% of our current packaging revenue comes from CoWoS and foundry business.

    *   **Technology:** It is a new, panel-based solution (310x310mm) with different requirements, including new technologies like camera mapping.
    *   **Competition:** Because it is a new area with different technical demands, TSMC has opened inquiries to a broader range of suppliers, including Brooks Automation and other automation makers.
    *   **Our Business:** We provide solutions for both CoWoS and CoPoS.

**Q: How does node migration, for instance from N3 to N2 at TSMC, impact your products?**
A: There is basically no change in product specifications for advanced nodes like 3nm or 2nm. However, we are seeing an increase in demand volume for wafer sorters. To improve yield on recent nodes, customers need to clean the FOUP (Front Opening Unified Pod) more frequently—every few steps in critical areas, rather than weekly. This increased cleaning frequency requires more wafer transfers from one FOUP to another, thus driving higher demand for wafer sorters.
