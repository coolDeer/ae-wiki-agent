## Technology: Optical Transceivers and PCBs
- **Market Outlook for Optical Modules**
  - Market Dynamics: There is no direct competitive relationship in the transceiver market. A new, significant potential market is emerging for Near-Packaged Optics (NPO).
  - Technology and Sustainability: The technical feasibility of 3.2T technology has been demonstrated, alleviating concerns about the long-term sustainability of pluggable optical modules. The market landscape for pluggable modules is expected to remain stable for at least the next five years.
- **Investment Thesis for Zhongji Innolight (中际旭创)**
  - Valuation: Currently at a very reasonable or comfortable level. Based on a 2026 forecast of 34 billion and a 2027 forecast of 46 billion, this implies a P/E multiple of 24x for this year and 17x for the next.
  - Undervaluation: The stock is considered clearly undervalued compared to its peers. For instance, Zhongji is at a 20% valuation level, whereas Tower Semi is at 70%, and US peers like Coherent are valued much higher.
  - Investment Rationale: It represents a good investment opportunity, possessing a reasonable valuation, good visibility, and a strong position in the AI sector amidst global supply chain tensions.
- **Recommendation on PCBs**
  - Top Pick: Tripod Technology (沪电股份).
  - Rationale for Recommendation:
    - It is making very smooth progress within the compute components of NVIDIA's next-generation server.
    - Its capacity plan indicates a very significant increase in AI PCB shipment volume for 2026 and 2027. Revenue is projected to grow from 200-400 million in 2025 to over 4 billion in 2026, and potentially into the high single-digit or tens of billions by 2027.
  - Industry Dynamics and Characteristics:
    - Unlike optical transceivers where Chinese companies are already first-tier suppliers, the PCB story is about the spillover of business from Taiwanese and American suppliers to Chinese suppliers.
    - PCBs are relatively more asset-heavy and customized than optical transceivers, which may result in a slower pace for generating profits and increasing volume.

## Financials: Banking Sector
- **Sector View and Rationale**
  - Preferred Sub-sector: China Banks are favored over insurance, fintech, and other financials.
  - Key Rationales:
    - Defensive Nature: From a macro perspective, the banking sector is relatively defensive.
    - Fundamental Recovery: The industry is entering a multi-year recovery growth phase starting in 2026. Q4 and full-year 2025 results were better than expected, particularly in Net Interest Margin (NIM) and asset quality.
- **Key Drivers for Recovery**
  - NIM Stabilization: The multi-year trend of NIM compression is ending.
    - Policy signals have shifted to a more neutral stance ("maintain a low financing cost environment").
    - Market data shows a slowdown in new loan pricing decline and stabilization in market-driven rates.
    - Forecast: NIM is expected to fall by only 3 bps in 2026 (mostly in Q1) before stabilizing, compared to declines of 17 bps in 2024 and 10 bps in 2025.
  - Liability Cost Improvement: Household funds are gradually shifting from high-cost time deposits to low-cost demand deposits ("demand-ification") as risk appetite increases, which will significantly lower liability costs for banks.
  - Attractive Dividend Yield: The dividend yield gap between the Big Four banks' H-shares and the 10-year Chinese government bond remains attractive, currently at the 10-year average level, making it appealing for southbound investors.
- **Stock Picks and Investment Thesis**
  - Top Pick: China Merchants Bank (CMB). Positioned as the biggest beneficiary of the reversing trends in interest rates and the shift towards demand deposits. It is viewed as a "comeback story" poised for a major performance.
  - Alternative Pick for Large Banks: Bank of China (BOC). Recommended for its more stable NIM, which benefits from its overseas business and foreign exchange operations.

## Consumer Discretionary
- **Sector Outlook and Investment Themes**
  - Overall View: The consumer discretionary sector currently lacks strong predictability and consistent, theme-based trading opportunities.
  - Key Selection Criteria: In the current uncertain environment, focus should be on companies with:
    - **Pricing Power:** The ability to set price floors in the mass market (e.g., Uniqlo) or command premium prices in the high-end segment (e.g., luxury goods).
    - **Resilient Demand:** A customer base with a high safety cushion in spending power.
    - **Favorable Cycle:** Being in a product or market upcycle.
    - **Internationalization:** Potential for growth in new markets.
    - **Shareholder Return:** High dividend yields are now a standard and attractive feature.
- **Top Picks and Conviction Levels**
  - High-Conviction Global Pick: Richemont.
    - Rationale: It has the strongest support from a high-value customer base, a valuation 25-30% lower than luxury peers, and a superior dividend yield of ~4%.
    - Catalysts: Recent results from LVMH confirmed strength in hard luxury (jewelry) and a recovery in US consumer demand, both of which are positive for Richemont. Its net profit CAGR is expected to be ~13% for 2027-2028.
  - China/Hong Kong Pick: Samsonite.
    - Rationale: While conviction is weaker than for Richemont, the stock offers an attractive valuation and benefits from the elasticity of travel-related consumption. The current stock price has already priced in expected weakness in Q1 and Q2.
    - Catalysts & Valuation: Potential catalysts include a dual listing and stable US demand. The target price is around 22 HKD, based on a 13x P/E multiple.
  - Defensive China Plays: Bosideng and JNBY.
    - Rationale: These are attractive "must-have" defensive stocks due to their low exposure to global markets, low P/E ratios (~10x), stable demand, and very high dividend yields of around 8%.

## Pharmaceuticals
- **Sector Outlook and Market Phase**
  - Market Transition: The pharmaceutical sector is at a transition point from a "left-side" (value-driven) to a "right-side" (momentum-driven) market.
  - Earnings Cycle: A major cyclical earnings rally is anticipated to begin around 2027, marking the start of a significant payback period for the industry.
  - Conditions for Rally: Three key conditions for a sector rally are beginning to align: 1) entry into a relative interest rate-cutting cycle, 2) potential fund flows from other peaked sectors, and 3) improving fundamentals.
- **Stock Selection Themes and Catalysts**
  - Key Themes for Selection:
    - **Globalization:** Companies with successful out-licensing deals and global collaborations are a key focus (e.g., Kelun-Biotech, Hansoh Pharma).
    - **Business Development (BD) Deals:** Identifying companies with products that are likely to secure a license-out deal with a multinational corporation is a critical line of inquiry.
  - Upcoming Catalysts: May and June are expected to be a period with an accumulation of industry catalysts, including a potentially positive data readout for Akeso's Harmony-6 study and possible BD progress from Hengrui and CSPC Pharma.
- **Investment Strategy and Top Picks**
  - Strategy: The recommended strategy is to "buy on dips" and accumulate shares in relatively cheap stocks, without chasing highs.
  - Top Picks:
    - **Conviction Call:** CSPC Pharma Group (石药集团) remains the top conviction pick.
    - **Also Recommended:** Hengrui (恒瑞医药) is worth watching. Akeso (康方生物) and its peer, Summit Therapeutics, are also considered good stocks.
- **Policy and Industry Environment**
  - Favorable Policies:
    - The upcoming implementation of the National Essential Medicines List is expected to expand drug volume *without* price cuts, which should help resolve hospital access issues.
    - New anti-bribery laws effective from May 1st are seen as a positive development for cleaning up the industry.
  - Earnings Outlook: Q1 earnings are expected to show growth, though the recovery may be gradual. Progress on the overseas front is more optimistic than the domestic market. The first half of the year is a key period to watch for catalysts.
