## FY2027 Guidance and Financial Plan
- **One-off Factors and Restructuring Costs**
  - FY2025 one-off costs: Included a 400M yen restructuring cost for inverters in China and an 800M yen cost for the robot business in Europe.
  - FY2026 planned costs: Includes a restructuring cost of just under 1B yen for the European business.
- **SG&A Increase in FY2027 Plan**
  - A significant 17.7B yen increase is planned.
  - Breakdown: Approximately half (9B yen) is for investment costs (5B depreciation, 4B development/reorganization), with the other half driven by increased labor costs (approx. 2/3) and other activity-related expenses (approx. 1/3). The company acknowledges the cost plan may be conservative.
- **Robot Business Guidance Rationale**
  - Profit is expected to grow, driven by a +6.7B yen value-added improvement. This is almost entirely due to the positive mix effect from the conclusion of large, low-margin projects in China and Korea, which reverses the negative impact from the previous year.
  - The volume effect is a -1.2B yen negative impact on profit, caused by the disappearance of sales (just under 20B yen) from these concluded large projects. The underlying volume, excluding these projects, is positive.
  - A return to higher profit margins requires an increase in standalone robot unit sales to improve factory utilization.
- **Motion Control Business Guidance Rationale**
  - The sales forecast of 280B yen is viewed as somewhat conservative given the very strong Q4 orders (79B yen).
  - This is because the company sees the current demand surge as "a bit like a bubble" and has incorporated the risk of this trend not continuing long-term into the plan.
- **Profit Decline in Other Segments**
  - The profit decline in System Engineering, Other, and Corporate/Eliminations is primarily due to increased depreciation from new facilities and the allocation of higher company-wide SG&A costs (personnel, R&D, restructuring).
- **Mid-term Plan Announcement**
  - A briefing for the new mid-term plan is scheduled to be held at the company's headquarters on June 1st.

    *   **Automotive**: A recovery is expected in fiscal year 2026 as pent-up demand is released, now that it's clear the future isn't solely about EVs.

It has AI built-in as a standard feature, allowing it to autonomously generate movements based on its surroundings (a digital twin concept), whereas competing products may use external AI with conventional collaborative robots.

**Q: What are the key one-off restructuring costs and income items affecting the fiscal year 2025 results and the fiscal year 2026 forecast?**
A: The main one-off items are as follows:
1.  **Fiscal Year 2025 (Year Ended Feb 2024)**:
    *   **Costs**: In Q4, there was a 400 million yen restructuring cost for inverter production in China and an 800 million yen cost for the robot business in Europe (brought forward from Q1 FY2026).
    *   **Positive Effect**: Business restructuring costs of over 1 billion yen incurred in Europe in FY2024 did not recur in FY2025.
    *   **Income**: Relocation of a systems integration base in China generated just under 500 million yen in income.
2.  **Fiscal Year 2026 Forecast (Year Ending Feb 2025)**:
    *   **Costs**: A new business restructuring cost of just under 1 billion yen is planned for the robot business in Europe.
    *   **Negative Effect**: The relocation income from China (just under 500 million yen) will not recur. This is roughly offset by the non-recurrence of last year's 400 million yen restructuring cost in China.

**Q: Could you explain the significant 17.7 billion yen increase in SG&A projected for the new fiscal year?**
A: The increase is split roughly 50/50 between investment-related costs and other operational expenses.
1.  **Investment Costs (approx. 9 billion yen)**:
    *   Depreciation: +5 billion yen.
    *   Development Expenses & Factory Reorganization/Relocation Costs: The remaining portion (approx. 4 billion yen). The plan is noted to be conservative in these cost projections.
2.  **Other Costs (remainder of 17.7 billion yen)**:
    *   Increased Labor Costs: Accounts for about two-thirds of this portion.
    *   Increased Activity-Related Expenses: Accounts for the remaining one-third.

**Q: How was the guidance for the robot business derived, and what is the path to improving its profit margin?**
A: The guidance reflects a major improvement in sales quality offsetting a decline in sales volume.
1.  **Guidance Breakdown**:
    *   **Volume Effect (-1.2 billion yen)**: This is primarily due to the disappearance of large-scale, low-margin projects from China and Korea, which amounted to just under 20 billion yen in sales last year. Excluding these projects, the underlying sales volume is actually positive.
    *   **Mix/Value-Add Effect (+6.7 billion yen)**: This improvement is almost entirely due to the conclusion of the aforementioned low-margin projects in China and Korea. The negative mix issue from these projects was fully resolved by the end of Q4.
2.  **Path to Higher Profit Margin**:
    *   The key to returning to double-digit margins is to increase the sale of standalone robot units.
    *   Recent years have seen more system integration projects, which have lower robot unit density per sale.
    *   Selling more standalone units would increase factory utilization rates (currently around 70%), which have been low due to weak orders while capacity has expanded, and this would have a positive effect on profit.

**Q: What are the assumptions behind the Motion Control sales forecast, and why are profits declining in other segments?**
A: The forecast balances recent strong performance with future uncertainty and increased company-wide costs.
1.  **Motion Control Forecast**: The sales plan of 280 billion yen is considered somewhat conservative despite extremely strong Q4 orders. This is because the recent order strength feels "a bit like a bubble," and it is considered too risky to assume this level will continue beyond the next six months.
2.  **Profit Decline in Other Segments**:
    *   **System Engineering**: A new factory will begin operations, increasing the burden of depreciation costs.
    *   **"Other" and Corporate/Eliminations**: These segments are seeing profit declines primarily due to the allocation of the company-wide SG&A increases, including higher depreciation, R&D expenses, and costs borne by the headquarters. The SG&A for the "Other" segment is planned to increase by 2.2 billion yen.
