BlackRock reports EPS upside while L-T inflows fall a bit short

BLK (BlackRock) reported solid Q1 EPS upside (+11% to $12.53 vs. the Street $11.48), with the beat driven by higher sales (revenue +27% to $6.698B vs. the Street $6.5B) and better margins (op. margins rose 130bp to 44.5% vs. the Street 44.1%), although long-term net inflows of $136B fell a bit short (the Street was modeling $146B) due to weakness in fixed income (FI inflows were $34.3B vs. the Street $55.3B). Despite all the anxiety around private credit, the company performed decently in this market (“private markets net inflows of $9B were led by private credit and infrastructure, where we have strong fundraising and deployment momentum”). CEO Larry Fink says this was “ one of the strongest starts to a year in our history ” as the company enjoys “ accelerating momentum , deep client engagement, and a platform built to compound across market environments”.
