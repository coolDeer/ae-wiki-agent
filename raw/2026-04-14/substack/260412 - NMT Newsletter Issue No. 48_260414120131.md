We had mentioned in prior letters and videos we needed to see a strong response off of the Bull Bear Line (6300) with a gain of +5.5%-8% which we now have printed! This gives very good odds the Bull Market is still intact. So we now get to check that box off! Whew! We had a +7.46% gain on the SPX from 3/30’s close to 4/10’s close now. Price low to Price high was a +8.37% gain in two weeks!
We did this with a 7th straight sessions with gains on the SPX. We had hoped for 8 the but got 7. We did though get 8 straight on the NASDAQ which actually needed it more (more on that below). This bodes well for forward returns on it’s own even without the SPX. I had posted in the Discord that Christopher Cadbury used to say this regarding 8 straight streaks….”Eight straight days of gains for either the Dow, the S. & P. 500, or the Nasdaq Comp. are generally bullish for the market despite many negative indications.”
We definitely have many negative indications right now.
But we also have a Follow-Through Day (FTD) in the structure here as well. This gives us very clear and defined risk. The lines drawn in the SPX chart tell us what those are. We have statistics that tell us where risk starts to increase and decrease. We want to see the FTD low of day hold closing basis for the most bullish outcome for the next 7 days (blue line at SPX 6740.28).
Holding closes above within the next 7 days also aligns with a Power trend being able to fire off and keeping lows of day above the 21ema as something else we will be watching. We can sweep below the FTD low of day intraday but really need to close above it. If we start trading 50% or lower into the FTD gap (yellow line SPX 6700) the odds start to increase dramatically the gap could fill and a close below the FTD is incoming and a FTD failure at that point.
So that’s our clear and defined risk going into this week. We have very strong action underneath us that suggests bullish levels hold closing basis despite many negative indications. That we pause and hold closing basis above the blue line. This means that the gap doesn’t get filled if this NASDAQ 8 straight and FTD on the SPX is solid.
