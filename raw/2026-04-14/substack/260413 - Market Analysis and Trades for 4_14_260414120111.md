COMMENTARY:
I feel like the bull gap held 😳😳😳😳
The strength of this market off the low can’t be denied. Since the low was made, only one day has opened above a prior day low and then traded below it and it ended as a bullish engulfing candle. Two days (including today) gapped down below a prior day low, and both were immediately bought. To reiterate from the plans, this active short time frame trend cannot be neutralized until a prior day low is taken out on a cash hours basis that does not end up a bullish engulfing candle.
That said, the market is clearly taunting sellers and likely me in particular with where it closed today.
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nWDs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff47be5f6-9e9b-41ca-87b7-26eacb247c4b_569x118.png)
NQ closed at 25538 and ES closed at 6921.25 (and floated up into 6926-6931 afterhours as NQ floated higher as well). Both indices saw a spike up into the close and it was absolutely enormous. This frankly looked like capitulation buying which is never a good look. Tomorrow is PPI which will be the hottest headline reading we’ve gotten since 2022, but the market is well aware. Even core is going to be quite hot, but again, the market is aware. Regardless of how bullish it looks, I still submit this is an area to be cautious. That doesn’t mean it is a short, but it isn’t the time to get bullish. VIX is into an area where hedging flows have continued to come in. The market has gone from extremely oversold to overbought in a short period of time and we hilariously have still not even had a chance to make a daily higher low as there has been no short time frame downtrend since the low. As always, we learn more from the first pullback than we do during the rally. Day trade longs are favored as long as the short time frame uptrend remains intact, new swing longs are best reserved for a proper pullback (though holding longs from lower is fine).

ES:
The base of today’s spike was 6900.25 within the 6898-6902 level and today’s high was 6924.75 just below the 6926-6931 level. A reminder that this is a yearly level (24-hour RTH only chart):

This is an enormous spike. Trading within the spike (between 6900.25 and 6924.75) is indeterminate whereas there is no lack of clarity in bullish continuation holding above today’s high (and the 6926-6931 weekly level).
Trading Higher:
Given the recent move, I do feel like an exhaustion gap up could be a risk but as long as 26-31 holds, bulls are in clear control and it is a day trade long. We would simply monitor the levels to the upside so there is very little else to say about upside other than head and shoulder tradoorrrrs (of which I am not one) would get very excited about a swing high being put in between here and the 6952 October high.
Trading Lower:
Holding back below today’s 6900.25 spike base looks like a rejection of today’s late day move and really looks like capitulation buying. Sellers still must ultimately still prove a willingness to put in trade below a prior day low to prove anything and that is nearly 100 handles from today’s high. However, we can at least back fill some of today’s structure and weakness can progressively creep in.
Below 6898-6902 targets a small set of single prints at 6990-6992. Given the amount of buying into the close this may or may not hold if seen. If not, then the weekly 6878-6883 level comes into play and I would think closer to 80. Holding below 6878 is where risk start to accelerate. This opens a potential move back to 6837-6846, just like reclaiming it put 78-83 into play from below. I would be mindful of 6864-6867 including a LBAF that could potential stall this move to 37-46.
We’ve seen a number of these huge poor structure trend days up this year. Some see some minor backfilling of structure before proceeding higher, some have been completely unwound, and of course we’ve had at least one see direct continuation higher. You never know what kind of mood the market will be in after PPI or what kind of headline hits, so while today’s low seems a mile away, holding below 6864-6867 puts 6837-6846 in place and today’s low just below there.
NQ:

Again, there was a tremendous amount of buying here and just above to start the year so caution is warranted on the long side. Trading within the spike (between 461 and 550 is indeterminate)

While I would be careful chasing a rip and think there are risks of an exhaustion gap, if a pullback from higher holds 516-539 then it is objectively a long. We will just follow the levels to the upside, and I get cautious again on any peek above 647 that finds responsive sellers as there was a very large set of single prints from 2/3 that led to the eventual break of 516-538.
Trading Lower:
Holding back below 440-469 looks like a rejection of today’s late day move and 369-388 comes into play which effectively includes Friday and last week’s high. Below 369-388 is where risks begin to accelerate (again, mindful that sellers have nothing until they put in traction below a prior day low).
I would perhaps be mindful of a LBAF of 369-388 which could come from as low as the 333 (today’s IB high) to 342 range. Holding below 369-388 and acceptance back into today’s IB (below 333) in particular likely sees 238-250 if not 210-218. Today’s low and the critical 111-145 level come into play below there.

TRADES:
Trades can generally be implied from the above. There’s nothing that I love as an amazing setup here. Bulls are safest holding above today’s high but I personally have no interest in being long above today’s high. There is some risk to buyers if they cannot sustain above today’s high (and certainly today’s spike base) but sellers have proven nothing so I’m not super keen on joining them. That said, my preference is to look for weakness and consider a short that aligns with the above. If there is no weakness, I won’t be short. Even if there is weakness, I will still keep a tight leash on any short until sellers prove something.
To me this might look like shorting a failure to sustain above today’s high and the 26-31 level, mostly looking for a test of today’s spike base and monitoring for continuation from there. If we get a gap down inside today’s range, then I’ll consider a short on persistent failures to sustain above today’s spike base, again keeping a tight leash on it.
If I end up taking zero trades tomorrow, I’m fine with it. I believe R/R is massively skewed to the downside from here, but buyers are in control of short time frames. Sometimes in these cases it is best not to trade or at least be ultra selective.
