### The Setup
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A9et!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3319f132-c07b-4429-98ba-6cb4620740f7_1256x768.png)
The big picture first. The S&P is back inside the 2.8% channel that held from October through February, before the war broke it. Friday’s close sits in the upper half of that channel. The supply band at 6,888-6,933 is not just a volume cluster. It is the channel ceiling. A break above it means the market has fully digested the war. A rejection means the channel top becomes resistance for the second time.
The intraweek behavior has shifted. For the first four weeks of the war, every week rallied early and faded into Friday. The last two weeks flipped: dip early, rip Friday, close near the highs. Both ended at +3-4%. The pattern changed from “sell the rally” to “buy the dip.” That is the behavioral tell the chart cannot show.
Price has carved out a tight range between 6,792 and 6,888, with the most contracts changing hands near 6,814. Friday closed at 6,865, inside the range and above the busiest price. Constructive, not a breakout. Both a pullback into 6,792-6,814 and a push through 6,888 are live.
Below the range. 6,758 is a busy mid-March price the market has never come back to. Magnets like that tend to get visited. Below 6,758 is a thin zone where almost nothing traded on the way down, so price travels through it fast. The first real volume shelf below is 6,675-6,705. A visit there is structure repair, not a thesis break.
Above the range. The ceiling is a band, not a line. 6,888-6,933 is where the top of the recent range meets the heavy long-term volume from the Feb break. A 45-point supply band that lines up with the pre-war channel top. Friday pre-market tagged 6,888 to the tick and stalled. That level is confirmed. Above 6,933 the zone thins out. That is a problem for later.
One thing the chart cannot show. Trend-following funds are well below one-year average exposure, hedge-fund gross is de-risked, retail was a net outflow last week. Systematic equity positioning is near cycle lows. Prime brokerage short exposure is at the highest level since mid-2023. The positioning stack is the cleanest it has been since the February top. That is the ammunition for a squeeze, sitting next to the earnings calendar.
There is a second thing the chart cannot show. 12-month forward earnings estimates are up roughly 8% over the past quarter. The S&P is down roughly 5% over the same window. Every other time since 1990 that estimates rose this much, price was higher, not lower. Either estimates are wrong or price is wrong. Given the positioning stack, the bet here is that price catches up.
The intraday data confirms the shift. 58% of sessions in the last 30 days opened with a gap down. The market bought every one. Average open-to-high: +0.63%. The buying is concentrated at the close, not the open. That is institutional accumulation, not retail chasing.
This is also opex week. Monthly options expiration pins price early in the week, then unwinds dealer hedges into Friday. The mechanical effect: dips get bought and rallies get sold until Wednesday. After VIX settles and the hedging flows clear, price is free to move. April seasonality adds another tailwind. Since 1928, mid-April (roughly days 10-16) has been the strongest stretch of the month. We are at day 10. The seasonal window and opex mechanics both favor the dip-buy through Friday. After that, the calendar turns less friendly.

### Policy and Geopolitical
The headline risk just became the opening print. Vance announced Saturday that talks with Iran failed after a full day in Islamabad. Trump posted on Truth Social hinting at a naval blockade and renewed military action. The Strait of Hormuz is still not reopened despite the Apr 8 ceasefire. Israel launched “Operation Eternal Darkness” against Hezbollah during the ceasefire, killing 254. Iran is calling this a violation.
Oil will reprice Monday. WTI dropped to $97 on the ceasefire. That ceasefire is functionally over. Saudi production capacity is already down 600k bpd from earlier attacks. Near-term oil is priced higher than later months, which means the market expects the disruption now but not forever. The gap risk is to the upside.
The geopolitical shock playbook still holds, barely. Since 1939, the median path for equities after a major geopolitical event shows a 3-5% drawdown in the first 25 trading days, then a recovery to pre-event levels by day 40-50. The Iran war has been tracking that median almost exactly. Friday’s close put ES right at the top of the pre-war channel. The mechanical part of the recovery is done. What comes next is either continuation or a re-test. The Vance collapse threatens to reset the clock and push the path below the median for the first time since the bounce.
The Fed backstop is conditional, not automatic. The March minutes showed most members would cut if the energy shock worsens, because employment still ranks above inflation. If oil spikes through $110 and unemployment ticks higher, the language shifts. A Monday gap down in ES with an oil spike is the scenario where I want to be a buyer, not a seller, because that conditional willingness to cut compresses the drawdown duration. The cut is not priced. That is the asymmetry.

Grade the dip live at the 6,792-6,814 test. Breadth: NYSE advancers above 55% on a red session, two +800 TICK prints in the first 90 minutes. XLF: green inside 30 minutes even with ES red. Semis: SMH/SOXX flat or green into an ES red open (if semis lead down, the market is front-running an ASML miss). Volume: low, stair-step, bid at 6,814 first (not high-volume one-way selling). Same-day implied range: under 35 bp means the dip is tradeable, over 50 bp means a tail is firing and you reduce size.
Five of five clean, full size. Three of five, half size. Two or fewer, skip the shallow level and wait for the deep shelf. The point of this checklist is to keep the highest-reward-to-risk trade (the deep add) on the board, not to get chopped at the shallow one.
SECONDARY (25%): Earnings absorb the headline. Bank earnings Monday/Tuesday are strong enough to offset the geopolitical gap. Price stays inside 6,792-6,888. Opex mechanics take over midweek. Carry what is on. No fresh ES. The leaders basket is where the money gets made in a pin week.
OUTLIER (30%): Hormuz escalation, flip short. Naval blockade headline drops. Oil spikes above $110. ES breaks 6,669 clean with breadth failing. Flip short on the first retest from below. Stop 6,712. Dealer hedging confirms the range. Call selling is concentrated right at 6,888 and extends into the supply band. The forced buying and selling from dealer hedges is real through Wednesday, but it decays after VIX settles and opex clears. This is not a huge expiration. VIX curve. Spot 19.23, Apr 20.11, May 21.35, Jun 21.73, grinding to 23.00 by October. The interesting number is the +124 bp step from April to May, and it is mechanical. April VIX settles Wednesday at the open. Thursday morning, May becomes the front-month, and the curve steps higher on that one print with no catalyst required. Friday monthly opex then clears the last of the forced dealer hedging. Post-opex, that mechanical support is gone.
Oil is printing the same shape as the VIX curve. Near-term oil priced higher than later months, anchored to the same deadlines the VIX curve is watching. Two curves in two asset classes saying the same thing is one of the higher-quality signals in this business. The VIX dropped from 36 to 19 in ten days while oil barely moved. After opex clears the mechanical support, those two may resync. If oil is still above $95 and the VIX is at 19, one of them is wrong.
Hedge selection. April VIX calls go away Wednesday. Any hedge meant to survive past the settle sits in May. The structure is a May VIX 25/35 call spread plus XLE and USO call spreads, carried through Friday’s close. Short ES is not a hedge in this plan. It fights the exact thing we are trying to own.
What flips The Lie back. Clean break below 6,669 with breadth failing. Oil gapping through $110. VIX curve inverting (front-month higher than second-month). Banks telling a credit story Tuesday.
