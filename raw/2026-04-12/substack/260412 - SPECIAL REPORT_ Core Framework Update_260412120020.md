#### THE DOLLAR
##### ✅ The Dollar bottomed in January with a Bear Trap reversal and big negative sentiment (even some magazine covers).
##### ✅ Now, the Dollar may have topped with a Bull Trap reversal at key resistance:
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bzfc!,w_2400,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F53065b0b-751a-4428-bacc-390af8b92bbd_1200x696.png)
##### Note a new tactical Sell is now active.
##### Near-term, DXY could bounce to the gap at 99.85 — but this is not required.
##### Big picture:
##### ✅ The Dollar rose against all major currencies during this crisis.
##### ✅ The Dollar even rose against traditional ‘risk-off’ currencies — most notably, the Japanese Yen and Swiss Franc.
##### ✅ It became clear from the outset of the conflict: the catalyst for a Dollar rally was U.S. energy independence, as a spike in Oil made terms of trade more attractive relative to other countries.
#### Consequently, the reverse is also true:
#### If Oil has topped — then there’s a case for the Dollar to have topped as well.

#### RATES
##### We came into 2026 tactically cautious on Bonds.
##### Today we’re considering a shift, for the first time in many months:
##### ✅ A peak in Oil and the Dollar would align for a peak in Yields.
##### ✅ There could be a transmission lag, but on the other side of this, Yields could have significant downside.
##### ✅ Signals support this:
##### For almost three years now, Yields have traded in a wide range:
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yMP7!,w_2400,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26b05fd1-81ef-4152-a0bc-e4097b0710e4_1200x675.png)
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2QPd!,w_2400,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7dcb55f9-9fc9-4033-85f3-d6275ea46d79_1200x675.png)
##### Yields have been in a mean-reverting regime since 2023 — every time Yields got overbought, they topped and declined for months.
##### If history repeats, the 2YR Yield could draw the following structure:
- An initial decline to ~3.60% where there is significant support.
- After that, a bounce to 3.90-4.00% to finish the top.
- If patterns repeat, a top would project a new cycle low for the 2YR around ~3% (a ~100bps decline from the recent peak of 4%).

##### In my view this setup is extremely compelling and timely.
##### Supporting this, Rates Volatility has already collapsed:
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aECC!,w_2400,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd34810f3-fb84-42cf-ad6e-f02db2cdb51d_1200x696.png)
##### The MOVE index remains in a downtrend since 2023, and has room to move significantly lower in time. (MOVE was often at 50-60 in the past.)
##### A decline in Rates Volatility is a positive signal for Risk Parity and Vol-sensitive funds.
##### As Rates Volatility continues to stabilize, systematic funds stop being forced sellers and gradually move to the bid side — providing flow support for the entire curve.
##### Previously, a collapse in Rates Volatility was a clear first step to a turn in Yields — planting the seeds for a bigger decline:
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z0SA!,w_2400,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4bb664f-1458-48b4-914d-160087d2a5a2_1200x696.png)
##### Those turns had a classic ‘transmission lag’ — from the initial collapse in Rates Volatility, to the eventual downtrend in Yields.
##### The same may be happening today.
##### If conditions align, 2YR Yields could be turning for a bigger decline later this year.
##### Such a move should be closely linked with a decline in Oil and USD.
##### We’ll be watching the next weeks closely, looking for a topping structure in Yields, as a big signal within the Core Framework.
