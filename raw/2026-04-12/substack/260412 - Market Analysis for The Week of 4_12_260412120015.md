COMMENTARY:
The short time frame uptrend remained fully intact this past week. Thus far it has been the definition of strength. On 3/31, ES dropped single prints with its 12:30 PM EST candle. Those single prints were defended on 4/2. On 4/2 fresh single prints were dropped during the 10:30am candle. Those single prints filled but held, producing this past week’s low (both Sunday night and Tuesday). A reminder from last week’s plan:
The above is effectively the definition of strength. The indices have yet to give us any reason to look for meaningful shorts. Based on Friday’s action, however, I saw early signs that this active uptrend may be ready to at least pause. This does not confirm until a prior day’s low is taken on a cash hours basis.
NQ has arrived at its major yearly level, and NQ has certainly led both the upside and downside for quite some time.
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zeO-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6898e9e9-e0da-4f2d-ba28-19ffd7bfea56_1811x787.png)
I would add that sitting just above this major yearly level is the “scene of the crime” area which led to this weakness to begin with. You may recall from the beginning of the year, it was volume build below 25516-25538 and 6894 ES that would (and did) open weakness. Both NQ and ES are very near those levels now. I can’t stress enough that this is not an area to be comfortable on the long side. For ES I am sticking with 6898-6902 as the weekly level but 6894 can probably stay on your own chart all year. While NQ can certainly fail at levels higher, any sustained strength above 25516-25538 strongly favors a retest of the current all-time high. The importance of this area (the yearly level and just above) cannot be overstated.

ES:
I will use 6837-6846 as our initial area of interest for this week. If 6837-6846 continues to hold (inclusive of a LBAF) then the uptrend remains fully intact and fading it (at least meaningfully) should be discouraged. As mentioned above, we are at the yearly level on NQ with its breakdown level just above. ES is approaching this year’s open and next yearly level (6917-6925). We will adjust with the daily plans throughout the week, but as of now this uptrend remains active above 6837-6846.
Trading Higher:
On a big picture basis, holding above the yearly 6803-6813 level opens a move to 6917-6925. The composite VPOC from the profile in my chart from the yearly plan sits right at Friday’s close 6855 (it can flip around between roughly 6847 and 6870). and is a decision point within that potential rotation.
ES 6894 should probably just stay on your charts but I’m using 6898-6902 as a proxy. The entire area between 6894 and 6930 has been very tricky this year and should be respected. If we got something like 6926-6931 on ES and 25516-25538 on NQ at the same time, I would become ultra cautious as a rejection there on ES can come back to 6803-6813, the loss of which really accelerated weakness to begin with. Regardless, we must keep in mind that in order for any meaningful swing high to form, a multi-day balance must break to the downside. There were plenty of “big spots” on the way up and we can see that shorting them blindly did not end well. Even if you doubt this rally, even bear trend rallies almost always go way further than you would think. I suspect many have already felt that.
I’ll just use summer of 2023 QQQ as an example, not to predict the same will occur, but to demonstrate how convincing bear trend rallies can often be. After breaking a multi-week balance to the downside, indices found a low, ripped higher reclaiming the prior multi-week balance only to fail into a lower weekly high.
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m2hW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ed47137-7e32-4009-8abc-6c3731616a8c_1816x541.png)
Back in 2022 indices even took out a prior weekly lower high two separate times, the bottom was called all across fintwit, and new lows were made both times.
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_xrb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4e776606-f606-46e4-9265-af97f0d54d9e_1825x741.png)
I’ll therefore be careful fighting an uptrend, but also careful assuming we are automatically going to new highs. The trading lower portion will be further developed and can be applied to any future situation
Trading Lower:
To start the week, we will be monitoring for any LBAF of Friday’s low (inclusive of a hold or LBAF of 6837-6846) as it has potential to put in a low for immediate trend continuation higher.
In order to neutralize this short time frame uptrend, we know that sellers must take out a prior day low. This does not start a downtrend; it simply pauses the active trend phase as long as it doesn’t end up in a bullish engulfing candle. More specifically, in this case 6837-6846 must be lost and flipped back to resistance sufficient for there to be acceptance (time and volume) back within this lower distribution pictured here:
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dq-E!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb9eac87b-12b7-452f-a8de-33f854b16b66_1812x533.png)
From there, once the daily has been brough to balance, the next decision is what determines if trend continues or if the market reverses into short time frame downtrend.
For simplicity’s sake, let’s assume that Monday follows the yellow path and comes down to or near Wednesday’s low. This would form a 4-day balance. The trades would then be this:
![image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_eZK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3926fe28-c9b3-468c-8b61-bd6f5d95fdd0_1799x800.png)
These are your basic box trades: An upside breakout with continuation is a long, a downside break with continuation is a short. A LBAF is a long and a LAAF is a short. The 6837-6846 range, at least for this week, will likely be a key decision point in all of that.
I generally expect to see at least 6790-6793 (and realistically Wednesday’s low and a test of the bull gap) if 6837-6846 is truly flipped to resistance. I will use 6790-6793 as a proxy for the yearly 6803-6813 level for this week. I will probably have 6803.5-6808.5 as a daily level again, but with the bull gap below I want to be cautious assuming that the yearly level has been properly lost. Keep in mind we can see a battle for a yearly level for many days as we have seen this year on both sides. As in the picture above, if we see the bull gap we would have a multi-day balance and watching for a reaction within the bull gap to trigger a LBAF of that balance.
In theory, acceptance within the bull gap has potential to fill it. However, that is an absolutely enormous gap. The most difficult situation (and therefore most likely lol) is traction into the bull gap that finds a low before it fills. The next move then becomes much more difficult, and we would cover with the daily plans. I believe it is difficult for sellers to reassert firm control until/unless 6654-6661 flips back to resistance and that is a long way away for now. This market has been nuts with headline risk, so I rule nothing out on either side.

NQ:
Really all of the above can be extrapolated to NQ. There is slightly less clarity in the two unique distributions formed on ES Wednesday through Friday but it is still there on NQ. The 25111-25145 level will be the major area of interest to start the week. Like for ES we will monitor for a LBAF of Friday’s low and we will also monitor for a hold or LBAF of 111-145 for direct trend continuation higher.
Trading Higher:
As mentioned above, I have absolutely no interest messing around on the long side near 25440-25469 and 25516-25539. That said, direct continuation higher speaks for itself and keeps bulls in full control of short time frame trend. I would expect 25669 to trade on any acceptance above 516-539. Generally, we’re just following weekly levels to the upside until the market finds balance on the daily timeframe (take out a prior day low on a cash hours basis as described above).
Trading Lower:
We will need to be on watch of a hold or LBAF of 25111-25145 (and 210-218 really). Acceptance back below 25111-25145 targets 24956-24969 and Wednesday’s 926.5 low. Below there sits the massive bull gap. Like for ES we would be on watch for a reactive bid within the bull gap to trigger a LBAF of whatever multi-day balance has formed. A multi-day break with continuation into the bull gap has potential to come down to 24452-24469 but we will need to monitor for a partial gap fill reversal.
Also like for ES, I do not believe sellers can firmly reassert control until 24369-24380 is flipped back to resistance so we may see some short-term lack of clarity on a partial gap fill that we’ll need to cover with daily plans.
There truly is not much more to cover for this week. To summarize:
-Friday’s high and just a bit higher was and is not the place to get comfortable on the long side.
-If 25111-25145 NQ and 6837-6846 broadly hold and trigger a clean break of Friday’s high then the uptrend remains intact.
-If we take out Friday’s low on a cash hours basis then the daily will be in balance. It is the break of that balance which determines the short time frame trend.
-The massive bull gap protects prospects for bulls and should be expected to be defended (perhaps a partial gap fill).
-If there is acceptance back below the bull gap, then bulls are potentially in significant trouble.
